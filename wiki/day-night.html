Day & Night

At a glance

One in-game day = 20 real minutes.
The clock (☀/☾) sits under the minimap.
A fresh game starts at 09:00.

CedarStory runs on a full daily cycle. Game time moves at 1.2 minutes per real second, so a complete 24-hour day passes every twenty minutes at the keyboard.

The schedule

Game timePhaseWhat happens
05:00 – 06:00SunriseThe world brightens; lamps, lanterns and windows go dark; NPCs wake and leave their homes; shops open.
06:00 – 18:00DayBusiness as usual.
18:00 – 19:00SunsetThe world darkens; lamps, stone lanterns, tower windows and hanok windows light up.
19:00 – 05:00NightTownsfolk walk home and sleep; shops board their doors; houses go dark and inactive; monster spawn rates double.

Night, in detail

  • Everyone goes home. Villagers, elders, kids, and pets head to their doorstep at dusk and vanish inside until dawn. Biscuit sleeps at Jonty's place. You can't chat with someone who's asleep.
  • Guards hold the watch. Hedda, the castle pair, and the keep patrol stay out all night — as does Mochi the cat, who answers to no schedule.
  • Shops close. Awning shops board their doors and unlight their windows; they open again at sunrise.
  • Monsters stir. Respawn waves normally land every 10 minutes per stage — after dark the clock runs twice as fast. The Wilds, the Undercroft, and Gnashfang Camp get genuinely dangerous.
Night farming: the Gnashfang Camp refills fastest after dark, and goblins have no resistances. Sunset in, sunrise out.

Real-time reference

Because a day is 20 real minutes, each phase transition recurs at fixed real-time intervals — sunrise comes 20 minutes after the previous sunrise, with sunset ten minutes after any sunrise. If you started at 09:00, expect your first sunset about seven and a half real minutes in.
