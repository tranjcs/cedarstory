The World

CedarStory is fifteen finite stages joined by gates — glowing circles on the ground, usually under an arch or at the end of a dock. Walk in and the world fades to the next stage. The minimap (top-right) shows the current stage, its gates (pulsing dots), and everything alive on it.

The Wilds

The starting stage: a vast wilderness whose edges vanish into fog. Five biome zones ring the meadow heart — the Deepwood (west), Frostreach (north), the Sunscar Desert (southeast), Mirefen swamp (east), and the Ashlands (southwest). Monster dens refill in waves; the road north leads to Willowbrook, and a war-scarred arch in the far southwest leads somewhere less friendly.

Willowbrook

A walled market town and the game's safe hub. The well plaza, Petra's stall, the river dock, and the General Store (walk in — the camera stays put inside small buildings). Gates: south → Wilds, north → Castle Town, west → the Farms, east dock → the Sapphire Crossing.

Willowbrook Farms

Windmills turning over furrowed plots, plus the full barnyard: chickens, cows, and two extremely patient oxen. Gwen and Rolf keep the crops alive. No monsters — just vibes and livestock.

Hyrmoor Castle Town

Stone streets around a fountain plaza beneath the castle. The keep's doors are finally open — the guards have opinions about your boots.

Hyrmoor Keep & The Undercroft

The castle interior: a columned throne hall patrolled by guards. A small door by the west wall descends into The Undercroft — a cell-lined dungeon where skeletons and drowned ghosts respawn endlessly. Bring fire. Or a friend.

Gnashfang Camp

A goblin warcamp off the Wilds' southwest arch: tents, bone piles, snarling totems, and more goblins than is reasonable. High spawn pressure — great for XP farming... if the game had XP. Great for practice, then.

The Sapphire Crossing

A Wind-Waker-scale open sea and the world's travel hub — you sail it in a red skiff that appears whenever you're aboard this stage. Reef sharks patrol between the islets. Docks lead to every far port:

Gullrest Isle

Palms, huts, a campfire that never goes out — and now pincher crabs skittering up the beaches alongside the usual slimes and one persistent bandit.

The Salty Curse

A derelict pirate two-master anchored mid-Crossing. The deck is all tattered sails, cannons, and ghosts — the crew never left. Healing magic sears them; so does everything else, eventually.

Arirang Village

A Korean folk village of curved-roof hanok, kimchi jars, terraced rice paddies, and jangseung guardian posts. Haraboji Ki will teach you a song if you stand still long enough.

Momiji Grove

A shrine town under permanent falling maple and cherry petals: torii gates up the shrine path, a nine-hundred-year pagoda, stone lanterns, and a koi pond of eleven (or nine) koi.

Merlion Quay

A gleaming port city — glass towers competing on height, a boardwalk esplanade, hawker stalls that smell incredible even in pixel form, and the merlion fountain spouting over its plaza.

Steamvale Springs

A hot-spring islet: three steaming pools among the rocks and bamboo, a little bathhouse, and Bertholt, who is at peace. Soak in the view; monsters don't come here.
